- The following exercises are designed to reinforce professional decision-making rather than tool memorization.

- Try to answer each question before reviewing your notes.

---

## Exercise 1 — Choosing the Right Scan

- You have authorization to assess a newly deployed web application.

- Before running Scanner, decide:

  * What parts of the application should you explore first?
  * Would passive scanning be sufficient initially?
  * When would active scanning become appropriate?

- Write a short investigation plan.

---

## Exercise 2 — Evaluating Findings

- Scanner reports:

  * 2 High severity findings
  * 5 Medium severity findings
  * 12 Informational observations

- Without performing any manual testing:

  * Which findings would you investigate first?
  * What additional evidence would you need?
  * Would severity alone determine your priorities?

- Explain your reasoning.

---

## Exercise 3 — Scope Verification

- You are preparing to scan an application.

- Create a checklist to verify:

  * Authorized targets
  * Authentication
  * Included paths
  * Excluded paths
  * Scan configuration

- Why is this checklist important?

---

## Exercise 4 — Validating a Finding

- Scanner identifies a possible reflected cross-site scripting issue.

- Describe how you would:

  * Review the evidence.
  * Attempt reproduction.
  * Confirm exploitability.
  * Decide whether it belongs in the final report.

---

## Exercise 5 — Improving Scan Quality

- Think about a large application containing many authenticated features.

- List several ways you could improve the usefulness of Scanner results before starting the assessment.

- Consider:

  * Exploration
  * Authentication
  * Scope
  * Configuration
  * Objectives

---

## Mastery Challenge

- Design a professional workflow that includes:

  1. Application exploration.
  2. Scope verification.
  3. Scan configuration.
  4. Passive scanning.
  5. Active scanning.
  6. Evidence review.
  7. Manual validation.
  8. Final reporting.

- Represent the workflow as either a numbered list or a simple flow diagram.

---

## Reflection Questions

- After completing the exercises, ask yourself:

  * Did I rely too heavily on severity?
  * Did I think about evidence?
  * Did I prioritize manual validation?
  * Did I consider business context?
  * Would my conclusions be defensible in a professional report?

---

## Skills Checklist

- You should now be able to:

  * Plan an effective scan.
  * Configure Scanner intentionally.
  * Prioritize findings logically.
  * Validate potential vulnerabilities.
  * Integrate Scanner into a structured penetration testing workflow.

## Key Takeaways

* Scanner supports informed investigations.
* Planning improves scan quality.
* Validation remains essential.
* Scope protects both tester and organization.
* Professional reporting depends on verified evidence.
